**To get cited by Perplexity, become the clearest, most trustworthy source for a specific question.** Perplexity is a retrieval-first answer engine: it searches the web, synthesizes an answer, and shows numbered citations for every response. You earn a place in those citations by publishing authoritative, well-structured content that directly answers the queries your audience asks — and by building the topical authority that makes Perplexity trust you.

**Key takeaways**

- Perplexity retrieves live sources and cites them in every answer.
- Being cited depends on relevance, structure, authority, and freshness.
- Its citation-first design makes Perplexity the best place to watch [AI citation](/blog/ai-citations) dynamics.
- You influence citations by being the most quotable, credible answer to a question.
- Track which queries cite you to see what's working.

Perplexity has become a default research tool for many people precisely because it shows its sources. For brands, that transparency is a gift: it's the clearest window into which content AI engines cite. This guide explains how Perplexity chooses sources and how to become one. Because AI search mechanics evolve quickly, treat the specifics as current-and-evolving.

## How Perplexity selects and cites sources

Perplexity works by retrieval-augmented generation. When you ask a question, it runs web searches, retrieves a set of candidate sources, generates an answer grounded in them, and attaches numbered citations to the claims it makes. Unlike a general chatbot answering from memory, Perplexity's answers are anchored to live sources it shows you.

That design means a handful of factors decide whether your page is among the cited sources:

- **Relevance to the specific query.** Retrieval is semantic, so content that directly addresses the exact question — in plain language — is easier to match and pull in.
- **Extractability.** Perplexity assembles answers from quotable passages. A clear heading followed by a direct, self-contained answer is far easier to cite than a dense block of prose.
- **Source trust.** Established, corroborated sources are favored. Authority you've built on your own site and across the wider web both count.
- **Freshness.** For anything time-sensitive, recent and accurate sources win.

You can't edit Perplexity's answer or see its index, but you can be the most relevant, extractable, trusted, and current source for the questions that matter to your brand.

## How to become a cited source in Perplexity

Earning Perplexity citations is disciplined execution of a few fundamentals.

1. **Answer specific questions directly.** Identify the real questions your audience asks and resolve each one with a clear answer near the top of the page. Vague, meandering content gives Perplexity nothing clean to quote.
2. **Make your content extractable.** Use descriptive, question-shaped headings, short paragraphs, lists, and tables. The easier it is to lift an accurate sentence, the more citable you are.
3. **Build topical authority and trusted mentions.** Cover your topic thoroughly and earn accurate references on reputable third-party sources. Depth and corroboration raise Perplexity's confidence in citing you.
4. **Keep your facts current.** Update time-sensitive information so Perplexity retrieves accurate, fresh data rather than passing you over for a newer source.
5. **Track which queries cite you.** Monitor where Perplexity cites your site versus competitors, and use the gaps to prioritize content.

These map directly onto the broader [answer engine optimization](/blog/answer-engine-optimization) playbook — Perplexity just makes the results unusually visible.

## Why Perplexity matters for AEO

Most answer engines hide their reasoning: you get an answer, but not always a clear view of which sources shaped it. Perplexity is the opposite. Because it cites sources for every response, it's the easiest place to observe citation dynamics directly — which sites get cited for which queries, how often, and how you stack up against competitors.

That makes Perplexity a useful testbed for your whole AEO strategy. If a change to your content earns you Perplexity citations for a target query, that's concrete evidence your structure and authority are improving — signals that tend to help across other engines too, even the ones that show less of their work. ## Tracking your Perplexity citations

Perplexity answers vary by phrasing and change as the web updates, so a one-time check tells you little. To manage your presence, sample consistently: run your target queries on a schedule and record whether Perplexity cites your site, for which questions, and where competitors appear instead.

[Elmo](https://www.elmohq.com/) is an open-source tool that tracks your citations and mentions across Perplexity and the other major engines, so you can watch your citation share trend over time. For the step-by-step method, see [how to track your brand in AI search](/blog/track-brand-ai-search).
