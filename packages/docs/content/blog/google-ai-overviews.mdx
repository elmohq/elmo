**Google AI Overviews** are AI-generated summaries that appear at the top of a Google Search results page for many informational queries. Powered by Google's Gemini models, an Overview answers the question directly in a few sentences and links to the sources it drew on — so users often get their answer without clicking through to a website.

**Key takeaways**

- AI Overviews are AI summaries shown above the traditional blue links for many queries.
- They run on Google's Gemini models and cite supporting sources.
- They increase zero-click searches, but being cited can still drive qualified traffic.
- You can't buy your way in — appearing depends on authority, structure, and relevance.
- Tracking whether you appear requires sampling, because Overviews vary by query and user.

## How Google AI Overviews work

When you run a search, Google decides whether the query benefits from an AI summary. If so, it generates an Overview using its Gemini models, grounded in content from its search index, and displays it above the regular results with links to supporting pages. They appear most often for informational and how-to queries and less often for navigational or transactional ones.

AI Overviews are one of three related Google surfaces. The Overview itself is a static summary on the results page. **AI Mode** is a separate, conversational search experience for deeper, multi-part questions. And the **Gemini app** is Google's standalone assistant. They share the same underlying model family, which is why understanding Gemini helps you understand all three — a distinction covered further in our roundup of the [best AI search engines](/blog/best-ai-search-engine).

Crucially, an Overview is generated, not retrieved wholesale from one page. Google assembles it from multiple sources, which means your goal is to be one of the trusted, quotable sources it pulls from — not to "rank first" in the old sense.

## How AI Overviews affect organic traffic

The honest picture is mixed, and worth stating without hype in either direction. By answering a query directly on the results page, AI Overviews increase **zero-click searches** — sessions where the user reads the answer and never visits a site. For purely informational queries that a summary fully satisfies, that can reduce click-through to the pages below.

At the same time, AI Overviews cite sources, and a citation in a prominent Overview can send genuinely qualified visitors who want to go deeper. The effect varies by query type: a quick factual lookup is more likely to end in zero clicks than a complex, consideration-stage question where users still want detail, comparisons, or tools.

The strategic takeaway is not to panic about lost clicks but to shift some of your measurement. Alongside rankings and clicks, start tracking whether your brand is *named and cited* in the answers themselves — a core idea in [answer engine optimization](/blog/answer-engine-optimization).

## How to increase your chances of appearing in AI Overviews

There is no submission form and no paid placement. Appearing in AI Overviews comes down to being the kind of source Google's models trust and can extract from cleanly. The practical levers:

- **Answer questions directly.** Lead sections with a clear, self-contained answer to a specific question. Overviews are assembled from quotable passages, so make yours easy to lift.
- **Structure for extraction.** Use descriptive headings, short paragraphs, lists, and tables. Clean structure helps a model pull an accurate snippet.
- **Cover topics thoroughly.** Address the related questions a reader would ask next. Depth and clear topical coverage signal expertise.
- **Build authority and entity clarity.** Consistent, verifiable information about who you are — reinforced with `Organization`, `Article`, and `FAQPage` schema — helps Google trust and resolve your brand.
- **Keep content current.** Update facts and dates. Freshness is both a quality signal and an accuracy safeguard.
- **Maintain strong technical SEO.** Overviews draw on Google's index, so crawlable, fast, well-linked pages remain the foundation. The same fundamentals power [generative SEO](/blog/what-is-generative-seo).

None of these guarantee inclusion, but together they materially improve your odds — and they are the same practices that help across every AI answer engine, not just Google's.

## How to track whether you appear in AI Overviews

Because AI Overviews are generated per query and can differ by location, personalization, and time, you cannot simply check a rank. You have to sample: run the queries that matter to your brand, repeatedly, and record whether an Overview appears, whether you are cited, and which competitors are named instead.
