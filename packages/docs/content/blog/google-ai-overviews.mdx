**Google AI Overviews** are AI-generated summaries that appear at the top of a Google Search results page for many informational queries. Powered by Google's Gemini models, an Overview answers the question directly in a few sentences and links to the sources it drew on, so people often get their answer without clicking through to a site.

**Key takeaways**

- AI Overviews are AI summaries shown above the traditional blue links for many queries.
- They run on Google's Gemini models and cite supporting sources.
- They increase zero-click searches, but being cited can still drive qualified traffic.
- You can't buy your way in; appearing depends on authority, structure, and relevance.
- Tracking whether you appear takes sampling, because Overviews vary by query and user.

## How Google AI Overviews work

When you run a search, Google decides whether the query benefits from an AI summary. If it does, Google generates an Overview using its Gemini models, grounded in content from its search index, and shows it above the regular results with links to supporting pages. Overviews appear most often for informational and how-to queries, and less often for navigational or transactional ones.

AI Overviews are one of three related Google surfaces. The Overview itself is a static summary on the results page. AI Mode is a separate, conversational search experience for deeper, multi-part questions. The Gemini app is Google's standalone assistant. All three share the same model family, which is why understanding Gemini helps you understand all of them. We get into that distinction in our roundup of the [best AI search engines](/blog/best-ai-search-engine).

One thing matters more than the rest here: an Overview is generated, not lifted wholesale from a single page. Google assembles it from several sources, so your goal is to be one of the trusted, quotable sources it pulls from, not to "rank first" in the old sense.

## How AI Overviews affect organic traffic

The honest picture is mixed. By answering a query right on the results page, AI Overviews push up zero-click searches, where someone reads the answer and never visits a site. For purely informational queries that a summary fully satisfies, that can pull click-through away from the pages below.

At the same time, AI Overviews cite sources, and a citation in a prominent Overview can send genuinely qualified visitors who want to go deeper. The effect varies by query type. A quick factual lookup is more likely to end in zero clicks than a complex, consideration-stage question where someone still wants detail, comparisons, or tools.

So the takeaway isn't to panic about lost clicks. It's to widen what you measure. Alongside rankings and clicks, start tracking whether your brand is named and cited in the answers themselves, which is the core idea behind [answer engine optimization](/blog/answer-engine-optimization).

## How to increase your chances of appearing in AI Overviews

There's no submission form and no paid placement. Getting into AI Overviews comes down to being the kind of source Google's models trust and can extract from cleanly. In practice, that means a handful of things:

- Answer specific questions directly, with a clear self-contained answer at the top of a section, so an Overview can lift it.
- Structure for extraction: descriptive headings, short paragraphs, lists, and tables that make an accurate snippet easy to pull.
- Cover topics thoroughly, addressing the questions a reader would ask next, since depth signals expertise.
- Build authority and entity clarity, with consistent, verifiable information about who you are, reinforced by `Organization`, `Article`, and `FAQPage` schema.
- Keep content current. Freshness is both a quality signal and a guard against repeating something that's no longer true.
- Hold up the technical-SEO basics. Overviews draw on Google's index, so crawlable, fast, well-linked pages are still the foundation, the same ones that power [generative SEO](/blog/what-is-generative-seo).

None of these guarantee inclusion, but together they move the odds, and they're the same practices that help across every AI answer engine, not just Google's.
