When someone asks an AI assistant "what's the best tool for X?", the model returns a handful of names from memory. **AI visibility** is the measure of how often — and how favorably — your brand is one of those names.

## How it differs from SEO

Traditional SEO optimizes for a ranked list of links. AI visibility optimizes for a generated answer, which behaves differently in a few important ways:

1. **There's no page two.** A model usually names a few options, not ten blue links. Being fourth is often the same as being invisible.
2. **Answers vary.** Ask the same question twice and the phrasing — and sometimes the recommendations — can change.
3. **Sources are blended.** A model draws on training data and, increasingly, live retrieval, so both your long-term reputation and your current content matter.

## What you can measure

A useful AI visibility program tracks a few things over time:

- **Mention rate** — how often you appear for the prompts that matter to your category.
- **Share of voice** — how you compare to competitors in the same answers.
- **Sentiment and context** — whether you're described accurately and positively.
- **Citations** — which of your pages the models actually reference.

## Where to start

Pick ten prompts a real customer might ask, run them across the assistants you care about, and write down who gets mentioned. That baseline — boring as it sounds — is the thing most teams are missing. From there you can see whether your changes move the needle.
